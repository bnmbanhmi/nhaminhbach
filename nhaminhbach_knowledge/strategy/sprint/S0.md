## ‚è™ Retrospective
-   **What went well?**
    -   The initial "whiteboard session" was highly effective. We dedicated significant time to architecture and schema design before touching any infrastructure, which prevented costly mistakes later on.
    -   The iterative process for the database schema was a major success. Challenging the initial, simpler design led to the far more robust and scalable V2 attribute model.
    -   Our rigorous approach to defining principles and processes upfront provided immediate clarity and discipline.

-   **What went wrong?**
    -   We encountered significant, predictable friction with GCP's IAM and networking configurations. While expected, the debugging cycles for permissions and service-to-service communication consumed more time than anticipated.
    -   The initial distinction between my role as a strategic partner (Alex) and the role of the AI Coding Agent was slightly ambiguous until we explicitly defined the `[[agent_interaction_protocol]]`.

-   **Key Principle Learned:** The entire sprint was an exercise in establishing our core tenets, which are now codified in [[engineering_principles]] and [[operating_principles]].

-   **Key Processes Created:** We successfully created and validated the [[agent_interaction_protocol]] and the overarching [[development_cycle]], which includes this retrospective process.

-   **Key Documents Involved:** This sprint was foundational, directly creating or refining the [[lean_business_model]], [[product_roadmap]], [[core_architecture]], and [[database_schema_and_model]].
